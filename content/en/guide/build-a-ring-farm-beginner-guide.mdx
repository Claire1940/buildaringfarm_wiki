If you are new to *Build A Ring Farm*, the biggest mistake is trying to expand too quickly before your core loop is stable. This beginner guide focuses on a simple progression path: secure consistent crop income, automate the first ring, then scale into high-value seeds and mutations.

## Beginner Progression Plan

1. Start with fast-growth crops to keep cash flow constant.
2. Upgrade sprinkler power and range before buying too many new plots.
3. Improve saw/harvester coverage so harvest cycles stay automatic.
4. Increase seed luck once your base income is stable.
5. Expand rings only when the current ring runs at high uptime.

This order avoids the common trap of owning more land than your upgrades can support.

## Best First Upgrades

The first upgrades that matter most:

- Sprinkler power: speeds growth and improves cycle frequency.
- Sprinkler range: reduces dead zones where crops idle.
- Harvester range: prevents mature crops from waiting too long.
- Seed luck: increases chances for better-value crops.

A strong early setup is not about rare seeds yet. It is about making every tile produce money continuously.

## Crop and Mutation Priorities

In early game, prioritize reliability over rarity. Mid game, shift into better-value seeds as soon as your automation keeps up. Late game, mutations become the true multiplier.

- Early: fast, low-risk crops for steady reinvestment.
- Mid: higher-value seeds with good cycle speed.
- Late: exotic/secret crops plus strong mutations.

When event boosts are active, save time-skip tools for your best mutated crops to maximize payouts.

## Layout Rules for Better Efficiency

Use a ring layout that minimizes travel and keeps sprinklers overlapping where possible.

- Keep harvest paths short and predictable.
- Avoid scattered pockets of unwatered tiles.
- Expand in controlled blocks, then re-balance coverage.

If your income drops after expansion, roll back and strengthen upgrades first.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

- Expanding rings too early.
- Ignoring sprinkler upgrades.
- Spending on low-impact upgrades before automation.
- Using boosts before crop quality is high enough.

Fixing these four issues usually doubles progress speed for new players.

## FAQ

**Q: What should I upgrade first in Build A Ring Farm?**  
A: Sprinkler power/range and harvester range first, then seed luck.

**Q: When should I expand to the next ring?**  
A: Expand only after your current ring has strong automation uptime and stable profit.

**Q: Are rare seeds worth it early?**  
A: Usually no. Build a reliable loop first, then pivot to rare seeds once upgrades can support them.
