## Pre-Requisites for Your Early-Game Rune Farm

Before we set out to amass a fortune in runes, there are a few essential items and locations you'll need to unlock. These steps are straightforward and can be completed very early in the game.

*   **Torrent (Spirit Steed):** Your trusty spectral steed is absolutely vital for this strategy, as it allows for swift traversal across vast distances and rapid escape from danger. If you haven't unlocked Torrent yet, simply visit a few Sites of Grace until Melina appears and offers you the Spectral Steed Whistle.
*   **Map Fragments:** While not strictly mandatory, having the map unlocked for Limgrave and Caelid (specifically Dragonbarrow) will make navigation significantly easier.
*   **Gold-Pickled Fowl Foot:** This consumable item temporarily boosts rune acquisition by 30%. We'll be using one at a critical moment to maximize our gains. You can find one early on by heading south from the First Step Site of Grace, jumping down cliffs, and then riding east along the coast to a pilgrim's body.
*   **Morning Star (Weapon):** This flail is crucial for our rune farm due to its innate bleed effect. Even if you don't meet its strength requirements, you can two-hand it (hold Y/Triangle and press Light Attack) to use it effectively.
    *   To obtain the Morning Star, travel southeast from the Agheel Lake South Site of Grace, following the road through the Weeping Peninsula. Cross the Bridge of Sacrifice (dodge the ballista shots by listening for them and rolling right), and continue south along the road. You'll find the Morning Star in a chest at the back of a broken-down carriage at a T-junction.

## Navigating to the Dragonbarrow Rune Farm Location

With your pre-requisites in hand, it's time to make our way to the Dragonbarrow region, where our target awaits. This journey involves a bit of travel, but the rewards are well worth it.

1.  **From Agheel Lake South to Third Church of Marika:** Fast travel back to the Agheel Lake South Site of Grace. From there, head directly north along the path. Ignore the turn across the bridge to the left and continue straight north on the dirt track until you reach the Third Church of Marika. Activate the Site of Grace there.
2.  **Using the Teleporter:** From the Third Church of Marika, look north towards the lake. You'll see a gap in the ruins. Head through this gap and down to the lake shore. Hidden in some bushes near the water's edge is a teleporter. Interact with it to be instantly transported to the Bestial Sanctum in Dragonbarrow.
3.  **Journey to the Sleeping Dragon:** Upon arrival at the Bestial Sanctum, you'll be in a high-level area. Don't worry about the enemies; we'll be ignoring them.
    *   From the Bestial Sanctum, look south. You'll see a golden tree in the distance – this is your waypoint.
    *   Mount Torrent and ride past the large enemy near the Sanctum entrance (it won't catch you).
    *   Follow the path as it curves southeast. You'll soon see a massive bridge with an equally massive dragon on it. Ride quickly across the bridge; the dragon is harmless and won't attack if you're fast enough (often, riding between its legs works best).
    *   Just before the bridge ends, on your left, you'll find a Site of Grace. Activate it.
    *   Continue along the path, heading towards the golden tree you spotted earlier. Ignore any lightning strikes; they don't deal damage here.
    *   You'll come across a series of large, glowing pots. Maneuver between them and then head left up a rocky incline. This will lead you to the Minor Erdtree.
    *   From the Minor Erdtree, head directly west along the cliff edge. Keep following this path, ignoring any enemies. You'll eventually need to drop down a ledge.
    *   Continue riding until you reach an area with a sheer cliff face. Carefully navigate up a small rock formation here. You'll see a large dragon on your left. Ride around its right wing, behind it, and jump over a small gap. You should now be safely behind the sleeping dragon.

## Executing the Dragonbarrow Rune Farm Strategy

Now that you're in position, it's time to put our **build a rune farm beginner guide** strategy into action. This method exploits the sleeping dragon's vulnerability to bleed damage and its inability to retaliate.

1.  **Positioning:** Dismount Torrent and enter sneak mode (crouch). Ensure no other enemies have followed you. Walk into the "armpit" area between the dragon's wing and body. This spot is safe, and the dragon won't aggro.
2.  **Applying Bleed:** Equip your Morning Star and two-hand it. Begin light attacking the dragon. Your direct damage will be minimal, but the key is to stack the bleed effect. Each hit contributes to a hidden bleed meter.
3.  **The Bleed Pop:** After several attacks, the bleed effect will proc, dealing a massive chunk of percentage-based damage (around 13,400 per proc). This will rapidly deplete the dragon's health. Continue light attacking, managing your stamina, until the dragon's health is extremely low – just a sliver remaining.
4.  **Maximizing Rune Gains:** This is the most crucial step.
    *   **Do NOT kill the dragon yet!**
    *   Once the dragon's health is critically low, open your inventory and use a Gold-Pickled Fowl Foot. You'll see an icon appear, indicating the rune boost is active.
    *   Immediately after using the Fowl Foot, land the final few hits on the dragon to trigger the last bleed proc and defeat it.
    *   With the Fowl Foot active, you will receive approximately 96,720 runes from this single kill!

This dragon kill is a one-time event per playthrough, but it provides an incredible early-game boost. You can then fast travel back to the First Step Site of Grace or any other desired location to spend your hard-earned runes. Depending on your starting class and level, 100,000 runes can easily net you 20-25 levels, significantly enhancing your character's power.

## Further Enhancing Your Rune Farming Efforts

While the sleeping dragon is a one-off, there are ways to further optimize your rune acquisition in Elden Ring as you progress.

*   **Golden Scarab Talisman:** This invaluable talisman increases rune acquisition by an additional 20%. When stacked with a Gold-Pickled Fowl Foot, you're looking at a massive 50% increase in runes from defeated enemies. The Golden Scarab is obtained by defeating the Cleanrot Knights in the Abandoned Cave in Caelid. This is a more challenging encounter, but the reward is permanent.
*   **Gold-Pickled Fowl Foot Farming:** Since Gold-Pickled Fowl Feet are consumable, you'll want a steady supply for future farming endeavors. These can be crafted using:
    *   **Rimed Flesh:** Dropped by Fowl (birds) found in various locations.
    *   **Rowa Fruit:** Abundant throughout the Lands Between.
    *   **Four-Toed Fowl Foot:** Dropped by Fowl.
    *   The Missionary's Cookbook [2] is required to learn the recipe, which can be purchased from Patches.
*   **Other Rune Farm Locations:** As you progress, other efficient rune farms become available. The "Mohgwyn Palace" bird farm is a popular late-game option, offering hundreds of thousands of runes per minute once unlocked. However, for an initial boost, nothing beats the sleeping dragon for ease and early accessibility.

Remember, a well-planned character build is just as important as having runes. While this **build a rune farm beginner guide** helps you get the runes, consider consulting character build guides to allocate your stats effectively and create a powerful Tarnished.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use this build a rune farm beginner guide with any class?**
A: Yes, absolutely! This method primarily relies on the bleed effect from the Morning Star, which doesn't require high stats. Even a starting character can effectively use a two-handed Morning Star to defeat the sleeping dragon.

**Q: How many runes can I expect from this early-game farm?**
A: With the Gold-Pickled Fowl Foot active, you will receive approximately 96,720 runes from defeating the sleeping dragon. This is a substantial amount for the early game.

**Q: What if I accidentally kill the dragon before using the Gold-Pickled Fowl Foot?**
A: If you kill the dragon without the Fowl Foot active, you'll still receive a significant amount of runes (around 70,000), but you'll miss out on the extra 30% boost. The dragon is a one-time kill per playthrough, so you won't be able to repeat this specific farm until New Game+.

**Q: Where can I get more Gold-Pickled Fowl Feet for future rune farming?**
A: Gold-Pickled Fowl Feet can be crafted once you acquire the Missionary's Cookbook [2] and gather the necessary materials: Rimed Flesh, Rowa Fruit, and Four-Toed Fowl Foot. Farming Fowl (birds) is a good way to obtain the unique fowl foot ingredients.
