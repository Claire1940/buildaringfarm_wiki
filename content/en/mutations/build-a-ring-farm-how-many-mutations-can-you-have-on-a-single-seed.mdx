<Section icon={FlaskConical} title="Mutation Limits Per Seed">
A common question among Build A Ring Farm players is about the number of mutations a single seed or plant can have. The answer is straightforward: **a plant can only have one mutation at a time.**

If a plant already possesses a mutation, it cannot acquire another one unless the existing mutation is first removed. This means you won't be able to stack multiple mutation bonuses on a single plant. Therefore, deciding which mutation to aim for on a particular plant becomes a strategic choice, especially for your most valuable crops.

This limitation encourages players to diversify their mutation strategies across different plants rather than concentrating all efforts on a single one.

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Note">
Mutations will not apply to your plants if you are offline during events. You must be actively in the game for the chance of mutations to activate.
</Callout>

### How to Remove a Mutation

Should you wish to change a plant's mutation or remove an undesirable one, you can do so using a specific item. The **Acid Spray**, available in the Gear Shop for $1 million, allows you to remove any existing mutation from a plant. This provides flexibility, enabling you to re-roll for a better mutation or simply reset a plant.

### How to Obtain Mutations

Mutations can be acquired in two primary ways: through random chance during specific in-game events or by purchasing and using corresponding sprays from the Gear Shop.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Event Participation">
    Wait for specific in-game events like Rain, Blizzard, Black Hole, Nuclear, or Galaxy. During these events, there's a percentage chance for your plants to spontaneously mutate. The rarer the event, the higher the mutation multiplier.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilizing Sprays">
    Purchase mutation-specific sprays from the Gear Shop. Each mutation has a corresponding spray (e.g., Wet Spray, Frozen Spray, Void Spray, Radioactive Spray, Rainbow Spray). These sprays guarantee the application of the desired mutation to a chosen plant, bypassing the randomness of events. Be prepared for high costs, especially for higher-tier mutations.
  </Step>

### Event vs. Spray: Which is Better?

While events offer a free chance at mutations, they are reliant on luck and event timing. Sprays, though expensive, provide a guaranteed method. For the most valuable mutations like Rainbow or Radioactive, saving up for the spray can be a more reliable long-term strategy if your luck with events is poor. However, early-game players might rely on events for the less expensive mutations.

| Method         | Pros                                    | Cons                                          | Ideal Use Case                                        |
| :------------- | :-------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Events**     | Free, can occur naturally               | Low chance, dependent on event schedule       | Early game, lower-tier mutations, casual play         |
| **Sprays**     | Guaranteed mutation, instant application | Very expensive, high upfront cost             | Late game, high-tier mutations, targeted farming      |
