<Section icon={Target} title="Strategic Layout for Mutation Events">
Designing your farm layout with mutation events in mind can significantly boost your income. While you can't force an event, you can prepare your farm to capitalize on them when they occur. This involves grouping plants, ensuring easy access for spray application, and having a clear strategy for which plants to target.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify High-Value Plants">
    Focus on your highest rarity or most leveled-up plants for mutation. These will yield the greatest returns from a multiplier. Group these plants together in an accessible area of your farm.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Prepare Sprays in Advance">
    Acquire the necessary mutation sprays from the Gear Shop (e.g., Radioactive Spray for $10B, Rainbow Spray for $1T). Having these ready means you can instantly apply a mutation when an event occurs or if you simply want a guaranteed result.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Monitor Event Timers">
    Stay informed about upcoming events. While the game doesn't always announce precise timings, active players often share predictions. Being online during these windows increases your chance of natural mutations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Apply Mutations Strategically">
    During an event, observe which plants naturally mutate. If a high-value plant doesn't mutate, consider using a spray to guarantee a mutation. Remember, a plant can only have one mutation at a time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success Strategy">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Managing and Removing Mutations">
While mutations are beneficial, there might be times when you need to remove one, perhaps to try for a better mutation or simply to reset a plant. The game offers a straightforward way to do this.

It's important to understand that plants cannot hold multiple mutations simultaneously. If a plant already has a "Wet" mutation, it won't acquire a "Frozen" mutation unless the "Wet" one is removed first. This emphasizes the need for strategic mutation management, especially when aiming for the rarer, higher-multiplier mutations.
